Ngan-Rachel Nguyen

Undergraduate Assistant

I am an assistant at the Stem Cell Core and Microscopy Core at the University of California Riverside. The Stem Cell Core and Microscopy Core are two essential places that are at the heart of research and new findings. The operations of these two Cores are making a difference in the world and new discoveries. I started off as a volunteer in Winter 2019, and was hired Spring 2020 till current time. As an assistant I complete any tasks or duties given. When on campus, I perform daily checks on machines, supply stock, and making sure everything is in tip top shape. I've also worked with microscopes and lenses. I have had to install and test out new lenses, and even test out a multimillion dollar microscope! My duties differ now due to the global pandemic, and am doing anything I can remotely. Such as taking training courses, and monitoring the stability of systems online.

Aside from my job, I am also a freshman mentor through the Freshman Collective Program. As a mentor, I help incoming students with the adjustments of college life. Therefore, aiding them with any advice or pondering questions they may have. As well as giving personal advice and experiences through my college career. It is especially difficult adjusting to college life online, and so I also help give tips on this transition and management.

I am also involved with a couple clubs on campus. I have been a member of the Student Homeless Aid Relief Project (S.H.A.R.P) for a few quarters now. The homeless population of Riverside is growing, and the mission for S.H.A.R.P is to aid those who may need help. With that we assemble hygiene kits full of basic necessities. As well as a brown bag lunch of water, a peanut butter and jelly sandwich, a granola bar and a banana. Later on in the week, we distribute these hygiene kits and brown bag lunches to the homeless individuals of Riverside. We also would make handmade cards during the holidays to spread some holiday cheer. Now that the club is remote, we make cards digitally for them. I am also a new member of A Friend In Me this quarter. In this club, we help bring pediatric cancer patients a sense of normalcy to their lives. Sadly, we are unable to visit the patients currently due to the pandemic, but the Pen Pal system was implemented this year and quarter. Through the Pen Pal system, we are able to write a letter for the patients and be able to communicate with them in this way. If it works out, we can then receive an email with a letter in return from our Pen Pal and continue that chain.
